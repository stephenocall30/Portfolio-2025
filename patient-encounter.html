Context & Problem

Urgent and Emergency Care (UEC) spans 111 online/telephony, Clinical Assessment Services, GP out-of-hours, urgent treatment centres, and emergency departments. Because these services run on different systems, clinicians rarely see a complete picture of a patient‚Äôs recent encounters.

The result: patients repeat their story at multiple touchpoints; clinicians piece together context from partial records; opportunities to spot deterioration, unmet needs, or patterns (e.g. frequent attendances) are harder to identify.

  • Align with NHS service standards, accessibility (WCAG 2.2) and clinical safety expectations.

Research

We spoke with 19 patients who had recently used 111 and 40 UEC professionals (from frontline clinicians to programme and technical leads) across England.

  • Patients: Most prioritised effective care over privacy concerns and expected clinicians to ‚Äúalready have their information‚Äù. Those with complex needs were most frustrated by repeating details.
  • Clinicians: Encounter history was more useful later in the episode of care (e.g. CAS/ED) and always interpreted alongside other clinical information. Regional maturity varied; some areas were close to solving this, others had little provision.

Process

Discover

Mapped the UEC data landscape (encounters vs episodes), identified boundary/integration gaps, and prioritised moments where encounter history adds the most value.
